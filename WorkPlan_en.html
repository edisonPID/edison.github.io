Working Plan

Objective 1: Characterize the different particularities of existing SDN solutions and how they impact

the detection of anomalous behavior.

The tasks carried out to meet this objective have been carried out during the first year of the project (June 2020 to May 2021) and have consisted of the study, implementation and validation of three infrastructures: simulated (mininet), virtualized ( type 1 hypervisor ) and based on containers ( dockers ). Various controllers and virtual switches have been deployed and tested on this infrastructure , focusing mainly on open vSwitch and ONOS. This prototype has allowed us to validate how the particularities of each solution impact the early detection of anomalies. This objective is considered 100% achieved.

Objective 2: Evaluate different alternatives for early detection of anomalies in SDN environments

The tasks related to achieving this objective have been carried out during the first year and a half of the project and will continue until the beginning of the third year, as planned. The public inSDN dataset ( https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2020.3022633 ) and CoAP-IoT have been used dataset , to verify the usefulness of different ML algorithms such as logistic regression , na√Øve bayes , random forest , adaboost and support vector machine. The experiments carried out using the public datasets have been repeated with the dataset generated with the infrastructure obtained in the tasks related to objective 1. It is considered, therefore, that the fulfillment of this task is 100%.

Objective 3: Define and validate new metrics in the early detection of anomalies

The tasks related to this objective have been carried out during the first half of the project. Work has been done on modifying the Early metric risk Detection Error (ERDE), previously used by group members in depression detection using real mean, to adapt it to anomaly detection. The dataset has been used inSDN and the public dataset based on the Vine social network, to validate this metric and the F latency , confirming the improvement in precision and sensitivity of the algorithms when taking into account the time in the detection of true positives. The objective is considered 100% achieved.

Objective 4: Analyze the different possibilities of interaction with the controller

The tasks were carried out during the first five quarters of the project, simultaneously with the tasks related to objective 1. Various alternatives were analyzed and tested with controllers such as Floodlight , Ryu , openDayLight and ONOS. Finally, the ease of implementing vertical services and the ONOS northbound API made us opt for this controller to address objectives five and six. The objective is considered 100% achieved.

Objective 5: Analyze alternatives for modifying network behavior

The tasks related to this objective cover the first seven quarters of the project, so they are currently in their final phase. Closely related to objective 2, the experiments carried out on public datasets allowed us to analyze the efficiency of acting on the control layer of the SDN network. It is considered 100% achieved.

Objective 6: Design and implementation of an interconnection system between the detection module

anomalous behaviors and the SDN controller.

This objective, dependent on the completion of objectives 2 and 5, focuses on studying the most efficient implementation of the models developed in the aforementioned objectives. Reached 100%.

Objective 7: Evaluate the behavior of the SDN network under anomalous conditions.

Depending on the completion of objective 6, it has made it possible to evaluate the behavior of the SDN network in anomalous conditions and obtain results for various indicators. Reached 100%.
