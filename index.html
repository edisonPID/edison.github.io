Project code: PID2019-111388GB-I00

Funding source: Ministry of Universities (Spanish National Authority for Scientific Research and Innovation)

The strong dependence on the information systems and communications networks of the current Information Society makes any threat, both voluntary (e.g. cyber attacks) and involuntary (e.g. device failures) have a high negative impact on a personal, business or organizational level. Network monitoring therefore becomes an essential part of system security to control its correct operation and detect possible security problems and network-based intrusion detection systems (NIDS) are the main tools for discovering attacks or anomalous situations based on the contents of the communication networks. Although different algorithms based on machine learning are collected in the state of the art that have good results in standard metrics for detecting anomalies, they do not contemplate the temporal factor in the detection, essential for early detection and preventing the anomaly from expanding its impact negative in the system and organization. On the other hand, the most important phase of anomaly detection systems consists in making decisions and performing actions automatically that allow to face threats or anomalies by modifying the behavior of the network. In this sense, software-defined networks (SDN) greatly facilitate this work by separating the control plane and data plane, allowing the integration of behavior and decision-making models. As the previous case, the state of the art includes different alternatives based on machine learning for the detection of intrusions in SDN networks, however they do not contemplate early detection. This project proposes the early detection of anomalies and intrusions in SDN networks to subsequently act on the control plane of SDN networks and modify their behavior to mitigate possible threats. For the early detection of anomalous behaviors in SDN networks, the use of machine learning from the processing of traffic captured in the infrastructure layer of an SDN network is proposed. Likewise, the metrics will be evolved to evaluate the early detection in the face of the disadvantages of the few metrics available, penalizing those alternatives with high precision but with a high temporal consumption. As a subsequent step, the development of a solution is proposed that, interacting with the SDN controller, modifies the treatment that the infrastructure gives to the data flows, with the objective of mitigating future threats based on the information obtained in the detection process performed. The research group, from the TIN2015-70648-P project Collective intelligence techniques for threat management in networks and systems carried out between 1/1/2016 and 9/30/2019, has developed the techniques for the study of the context, extraction of main characteristics, adaptation of classification and prediction algorithms, optimization and analysis of results that will serve as the main basis to successfully address this project oriented to a different environment (SDN networks), but did not contemplate the temporal aspect of the early detection of intrusions and anomalies.
